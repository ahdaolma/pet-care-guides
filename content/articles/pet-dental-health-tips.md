## Daily Dental Care Checklist

- [ ] Brush teeth with enzymatic pet toothpaste (CET Virbac $9 or Petsmile $15-$25 with VOHC seal)
- [ ] Angle brush at 45° to gum line, focus on outer surfaces of upper teeth
- [ ] 30-60 seconds total — quality over duration
- [ ] Reward with high-value treat after brushing
- [ ] Check gums for redness, swelling, or bleeding
- [ ] Check breath — persistent foul smell = bacterial overgrowth = vet visit

## Weekly/Monthly Dental Tasks

- [ ] Inspect all teeth for brown/yellow tartar buildup, chips, or looseness
- [ ] Check for retained baby teeth in puppies/kittens under 6 months
- [ ] Replace toothbrush every 3 months (bristles wear down, bacteria accumulate)
- [ ] Give VOHC-approved dental chew 2-3x weekly (Greenies, Virbac C.E.T. VeggieDent)
- [ ] Refresh water additive (Oxyfresh or TropiClean) if using

## Warning Signs: When to See the Vet

**Urgent (same-day vet visit):**
- Swelling under the eye (tooth root abscess — can spread to the brain)
- Bleeding from the mouth that doesn't stop
- Broken tooth exposing pink/red pulp inside
- Inability to close the mouth (possible jaw fracture from advanced bone loss)

**Soon (schedule within 1 week):**
- Brown crusty tartar covering more than 30% of teeth
- Gums bleed when touched lightly
- Loose teeth in an adult pet
- Dropping food or chewing on only one side
- Sudden preference for wet food over dry
- Pawing at the mouth repeatedly

**Monitor (improve home care, mention at next checkup):**
- Mild bad breath (improve with brushing, recheck in 2 weeks)
- Slight redness along gum line
- One or two small tartar spots

## DO NOT Use These

- **Human toothpaste** — fluoride causes acute toxicity; xylitol causes liver failure in dogs (fatal at 0.05g per pound of body weight)
- **Raw bones, antlers, hooves** — #1 cause of slab fractures ($300-$800 extraction)
- **Hard nylon bones for aggressive chewers** — tooth fracture risk
- **Any chew you can't indent with a thumbnail** — too hard for enamel
- **Anesthesia-free dentistry** — condemned by AVDC and AAHA; leaves subgingival disease untreated
- **Salt water or hydrogen peroxide rinses** — if swallowed, causes vomiting and potential esophageal damage

## The 30-Second Daily Routine

**Morning or evening, whichever you'll actually do consistently:**

1. **Gather:** Toothpaste on finger brush or toothbrush (10 seconds prep)
2. **Lift lip:** Expose upper canine and premolars (2 seconds)
3. **Brush outer surfaces:** Small circles at gum line, upper teeth first, then lower (20 seconds)
4. **Reward:** High-value treat + praise (5 seconds)
5. **Done.** 30-60 seconds. Every 24 hours. That's the difference between a dog who dies with all its teeth and a dog who has 12 extractions by age 8.
