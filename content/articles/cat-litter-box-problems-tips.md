## 10 Tips to Fix Cat Litter Box Problems

![10 Tips to Fix Cat Litter Box Problems](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse3.explicit.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.rBgdPMOSpd78vIcqdXSCEQHaNM%3Fr%3D0%26pid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=3b19a70cc6ae3fd9fdb6bdf9b67df3a5cd41c6808a6c0227e7c6c8e963d402de&ipo=images)


### 1. Vet Visit First — 55% of Cases Are Medical

UTI, bladder stones, kidney disease, diabetes, arthritis — all cause litter box avoidance. A urinalysis costs $40-$80. Replacing urine-soaked carpet costs $2,000+. Vet first, behavior second.

### 2. Number of Boxes = Number of Cats + 1

One cat = 2 boxes. Two cats = 3 boxes. Spread across different rooms and floors. Basement-only boxes get ignored. Each level of the house needs a box.

### 3. Open Boxes, Not Covered

Covered boxes trap ammonia. Cats smell 14× better than humans. A covered box that smells fine to you is a gas chamber to them. Open boxes. Scoop twice daily. Unscented litter only.

### 4. Feliway Diffuser Reduces Marking by 70%

Feliway (synthetic feline facial pheromone) signals "this is safe territory." Clinical trials show a 70% reduction in stress-related marking. Place diffusers in rooms where accidents occurred. Replace refills every 30 days.

### 5. Enzyme Cleaner on Every Accident Site

Regular cleaners remove odor for humans but not cats. The cat smells their own urine and returns to the spot. Saturate with enzyme cleaner (Nature's Miracle, Anti-Icky-Poo). Let sit 10-15 minutes. Blot. Repeat for deep-penetrated urine (carpet pad).

### 6. Never Punish — It Makes It Worse

Rubbing the cat's nose in it, yelling, spraying water — these teach the cat to fear YOU, not to use the box. The cat may start hiding their accidents (behind furniture, in closets) instead of using the box. Punishment is scientifically proven to worsen house-soiling.

### 7. Confine to One Room for 7-14 Days

The 30-day retraining protocol starts with confinement to a small space with a clean box. The cat learns: box = only option. After 7 days of consistent use, expand territory gradually. Add a second box in the new area.

### 8. Box Size = 1.5× Cat Length

Kitten-sized boxes do not work for 15-pound cats. The box should be long enough for the cat to turn around comfortably. For seniors: cut down one side to 2-3 inches for easy entry.

### 9. Separate Food and Litter Areas

Cats do not eliminate where they eat. If the food bowl is 3 feet from the box, the cat may choose a different corner entirely. Food and litter should be in different rooms when possible.

### 10. Arthritis in Senior Cats Is a Litter Box Problem

A 12-year-old cat with arthritis experiences pain climbing into a high-sided box. They associate the box with pain and stop using it. Switch to a low-entry senior box. Treat the arthritis (Cosequin, adequan injections, pain medication). Litter box problems in seniors are often undiagnosed pain.
