## 10 Tips to Design a Home That Survives Dogs and Cats

![Pet-friendly home tips](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse2.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.3yHnWLrfiyPjXaNMyPmgrwHaHa%3Fpid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=b0e7120f38cd426086cc5dbf4d68a071034b02eb9918ce44c784ee655fff994a&ipo=images)

### 1. Luxury Vinyl Plank Is the Best Flooring for Pets — Period

LVP with a 20+ mil wear layer is waterproof, scratch-resistant, warmer than tile, and costs $3-$7/sq ft installed. Urine, vomit, and muddy paw prints wipe clean. Unlike hardwood (which stains black from urine and scratches from every nail), LVP survives a decade of large dogs. The waterproof core means liquid accidents never penetrate to the subfloor. If you are replacing flooring in a pet home, this is the answer.

### 2. Ruggable Washable Rugs: When (Not If) an Accident Happens, Strip and Wash

Traditional area rugs absorb urine into the pad — the stain is surface-cleaned but the odor persists forever, encouraging the pet to re-mark. Ruggable rugs ($100-$400) have a removable, machine-washable cover and a non-slip pad. When the dog vomits or the cat misses the litter box, strip the cover, throw it in cold wash, and the rug is clean in 90 minutes. One Ruggable pays for itself in 2-3 avoided professional carpet cleanings.

### 3. Performance Velvet Outlasts Every Other Upholstery Fabric

Traditional velvet is delicate. Performance velvet (Crypton, Sunbrella, Inside Out) is rated for 100,000+ double-rubs — hospital-grade durability. It is liquid-resistant, stain-proof, and bleach-cleanable. Claws do not catch the tight pile (unlike woven linen or boucle). Pet hair does not embed — a lint roller removes all fur in 30 seconds. Performance velvet sofas from Article, Joybird, or Room & Board cost $1,500-$3,500 and survive 10+ years of pets.

### 4. Install Wall-Mounted Sisal Panels — Your Cat Will Scratch These Instead of the Sofa

Cover a 2×4 foot section of wall with sisal fabric stretched over a wooden frame. Mount at cat shoulder height near the furniture the cat currently scratches. The cat scratches the sisal (which feels BETTER than upholstery) instead of the $2,000 sofa. Cost: $30-$50 DIY. Alternatively: wrap a staircase stringer with sisal rope ($20 for 100 feet). The cat has a floor-to-ceiling scratching surface and the staircase looks custom-designed.

### 5. Remove ALL Lilies from a Home with Cats

Lilies (Lilium, Hemerocallis — Easter lily, Tiger lily, Daylily, Stargazer) are catastrophically toxic to cats. Every part of the plant — petals, leaves, pollen, even the water in the vase — causes acute kidney failure within 24-72 hours. A cat that licks lily pollen off its fur after brushing against a flower can die. If you own cats, lilies do not enter your home. Period. The ASPCA reports lily toxicity cases spike every Easter and Mother's Day.

### 6. Leather Furniture + Throw Blankets = The Easiest Pet Setup

Protected leather (not aniline — that absorbs everything) is naturally claw-resistant and wipes clean. Pet hair does not embed — it slides off. The "patina" from scratches is considered desirable by some. Add machine-washable throw blankets over the seat cushions (where dogs actually lie) and wash weekly. The blanket takes the wear; the leather lasts decades. A leather sofa + 3-4 cheap fleece throws is simpler and more durable than any performance fabric sofa alone.

### 7. Cat Shelves Turn Dead Wall Space into Territory

Wall-mounted cat shelves ($15-$25 each) spaced 12-18 inches apart vertically create an elevated pathway. A cat traversing a room at shelf height has dramatically more territory and significantly less stress than a floor-bound cat. Stressed cats urine-mark and scratch furniture. The "cat superhighway" is not an aesthetic indulgence — it is stress-prevention infrastructure. Install shelves in the main living area and near windows for bird watching.

### 8. Choose Paint with a Satin or Semi-Gloss Finish for Wipeable Walls

Flat/matte paint absorbs drool, muddy paw smears, and the mysterious brown substance dogs rub on walls. It cannot be cleaned — scrubbing removes the paint. Eggshell is barely better. Satin or semi-gloss finish ($40-$60/gallon) wipes clean with a damp cloth. Use this finish in hallways, mudrooms, and any wall below waist height. For extreme durability: kitchen and bathroom paint (Benjamin Moore Aura Bath & Spa, Sherwin-Williams Emerald) resists moisture, stains, and scrubbing.

### 9. Hide the Litter Box in Plain Sight — Cabinet Conversions

Remove the toe-kick from a bathroom vanity or laundry room cabinet. Cut a cat-sized entry hole (6-8 inches diameter). The litter box lives inside, invisible to guests. Add a motion-activated LED light ($10). Guests see a normal cabinet; the cat has a private bathroom. Alternative: convert the wasted triangular space under a staircase with a cat door in the wall. The litter box lives in dead space no human was using, completely contained and odor-managed.

### 10. A Mudroom or "Dog Station" Near the Entry Door Prevents 80% of Dirt Indoors

A simple setup by the door you use for walks: a boot tray ($15) for muddy paws, a microfiber towel for paw wiping, a sealed bin for leashes/harnesses/poop bags, and a washable mat. Wipe paws and bellies before the dog enters the main house. This single habit prevents the muddy paw prints that travel from the door, across the kitchen, onto the living room rug. If you have space: a pet wash station (utility sink or walk-in shower on the ground floor near the door) is the ultimate upgrade — rinse muddy dogs without carrying them through the house.
